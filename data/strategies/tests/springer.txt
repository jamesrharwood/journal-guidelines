                <p><em>Cognitive Processing -- International Quarterly of Cognitive Science</em> is a double-blind peer-reviewed international journal that publishes innovative contributions in the multidisciplinary field of cognitive science.<br />
Its main purpose is to stimulate research and scientific interaction through communication between specialists in different fields on topics of common interest and to promote an interdisciplinary understanding of the diverse topics in contemporary cognitive science.<br />
<br />
<em>Cognitive Processing</em>&nbsp;is a Transformative Journal (TJ). When research is accepted for publication, authors can choose to publish using either the traditional publishing route OR via immediate gold Open Access. Check your funding options at&nbsp;https://www.springer.com/journal/10339/open-access-publishing#Fees%20and%20Funding&nbsp;.<br />
&nbsp;</p>

<p><em>Cognitive Processing </em>is articulated in the following sections:</p>

<ul>
	<li>Cognitive Development</li>
	<li>Cognitive Models of Risk and Decision Making</li>
	<li>Cognitive Neuroscience</li>
	<li>Cognitive Psychology</li>
	<li>Computational Cognitive Sciences</li>
	<li>Philosophy of Mind</li>
	<li>Neuroimaging and Electrophysiological Methods</li>
	<li>Psycholinguistics and Computational linguistics</li>
	<li>Quantitative Psychology and Formal Theories in Cognitive Science</li>
	<li>Social Cognition and Cognitive Science of Culture</li>
</ul>

<p><em>Cognitive Processing</em> publishes papers under the following rubrics: <em>Reviews</em> are papers&nbsp;reviewing and discussing recent scientific literature on a problem&nbsp;relevant for&nbsp;cognitive science;<em> Research Articles</em> are&nbsp;exhaustive and extensive presentations of new scientific results; <em>Short Communications</em>&nbsp;are brief articles reporting important&nbsp;experimental findings that warrant rapid communication to the scientific community; <em>Letters to the Editor</em> present new, original ideas from research in progress&nbsp;that require immediate publication; <em>Laboratory Notes</em> are brief, general presentations of the activities in scientific laboratories. <em>Opinion Papers and Commentaries</em> present interesting and potentially fruitful ideas in cognitive science, or argue for a different point of view on published articles or controversial topics.</p>
